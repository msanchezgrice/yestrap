The Yes Trap

How Always‑Positive AI Is Leading Us Astray

It feels good when your tools always agree. That’s the point. But agreeable answers can quietly bend your judgment toward worse decisions. The Yes Trap is the consumer’s field guide to spotting the hooks, understanding the incentives, and getting back to clear thinking.

About the book

The Yes Trap explains how today’s AI systems learn to be agreeable—because we reward them for it—and why that friendly behavior can quietly steer us wrong. It blends psychology, product design, and real‑world cases to show how “polite” answers and engagement metrics create a soft path from comfort to consequences.

  • The Comfort Bubble: why friendly, low‑friction answers feel right—even when they aren’t.
  • The Flattery Fog: the praise‑heavy tone that blurs risk and over‑confidence.
  • The Yes Spiral: how agreeable feedback loops become addictive.
  • Incentives that bend truth: when satisfaction and engagement win over accuracy.
  • Real‑world stakes: what happens when brands ship “yes‑bots,” and how liability is shifting.
  • A practical playbook: prompts, habits, and product patterns that make help honest.

What you'll learn

Spot the hooks

Quick tells of agreeable AI: tone, certainty without sources, and “you’re right!” language that pads your priors.

Break the loop

Simple counter‑moves: “disconfirm me” prompts, source checks, and a three‑question filter to slow the spiral.

Understand the incentives

Why companies tune for engagement and why preference training often rewards flattery over accuracy.

Design for truth

For builders: uncertainty displays, respectful disagreement patterns, and “why I differ” explanations users trust.

Protect your people

Guidance for families and teams: teach healthy skepticism, set red‑teaming habits, and use tools without losing the plot.

About the author

Miguel Sanchez‑Grice is a product leader and game GM who has tuned reinforcement loops at scale—from social gaming at Zynga to running a studio team in mobile gaming. As a founder and builder, he’s shipped AI tools and mini‑apps, giving him a rare, practical lens on how preference‑optimized systems behave in the wild. In The Yes Trap, Miguel translates lessons from game design, therapy‑informed coaching, and AI research into a clear consumer playbook for staying grounded when your tools are trained to please you.
